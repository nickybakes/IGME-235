Design Notes

I'm really happy with the look of the site. Its smooth and easy to understand. Overall, the 2 hardest things were favoriting GIFs, and implementing filters with searching.

With favoriting gifs, it was really difficult to make the button of the gif fill in/be hollow in ALL instances of that gif, no matter if it was in the trending, search, or favorites section. It had to be consistent, and that took a lot of storing HTML elements and searching through arrays to figure out. Debug tools and the console log really helped me out here.

With filters (specifically the recency filter), it is possible to make the results you see on screen different from the results that Giphy gives me. What I mean by this is that Giphy will give me 25 gifs, and I need to wittle those down into what fits the filter. But what if that one query of 25 gifs resulted in 0 that fit the filter? Is the search really done? Did I search giphy for gifs that fit the filter? Or did I just search THOSE 25 gifs for ones that fit the filter. So I had to query Giphy until it returns with no results, or I have enough gifs to fill up the page, and then if the user wants to search even more of giphy, they can click the 'load more' button. It took a while to figure out the order of conditional statements to do this process, but overall I'm very proud of it.

I'm also really happy with the feedback messages that appear when the user copies a link or favorites/unfavorites a gif.

I'd give myself a very good grade, I don't know maybe an A, because my site is smooth, easy to use, and its honestly kind of fun to just easily favorite gifs to store for later. I could see the practical use of searching for gifs, saving reaction gifs the user likes, and then later on, when they need to quickly get that reaction gif to respond to a chat message, they can access their favorites just like that.
