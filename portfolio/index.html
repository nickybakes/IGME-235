Projects

Scarlet Meadow

Feb 2020 - May 2020

A 2D platformer, beat-em-up game with traditional, hand-drawn animation, developed in C# and Monogame. I worked with a team as the lead artist, developed a physics system for objects in the game, and designed and developed a robust, graphical level editor using C# and Monogame for content production for the game. The game features traditional, hand-drawn animation with inspiration from comic books and Cuban architecture.

Download the game and level editor

Cursed Cove

Apr 2018 - Oct 2018

In a team of 12, I helped developed the map "Cursed Cove" for Valve Software's "Team Fortress 2". The map was bought and featured by Valve as a part of the Halloween "Scream Fortress" event in 2018. On the team, I helped bring up concepts for the level design, and I developed parts of the background/skybox of the map.

View on Steam Workshop

Queen Elizabeth III in "A Royal Pain in the Arse"

Feb 2018 - Apr 2018

A Team Fortress 2 map featuring a boss fight with a traditionally, hand-drawn animated character inspired by 1930's cartoons. The map also features many other gimmicks including minigames, unlockable power-ups, and many references and jokes.

View animation reel

Neon Pong

Apr 2019 - May 2019

A side-scrolling, singleplayer game where you return attacks from a "Pong" invasion in urban environments. The game was developed using Java, utilizing built-in Java libraries, and it features traditional, hand-drawn animation and a bold comic book style. "Neon Pong" stars two characters, Electra and Suit, from "Neon Heights" (see Ongoing Projects).

Download the game

Ongoing Projects

Neon Heights

Feb 2019 - Current

A local and online multiplayer game currently being developed in Unity. Two teams of four players pick between eight different unique characters to play in this intense Team Hand Ball inspired sport, but with more platforming, fighting, and teamwork. Teams will face off in sprawling, urban environments such as neon-lit rooftops and overgrown underpasses. Prototypes of the game were developed with character and level designs, however an updated, online version of the game is still in development.
